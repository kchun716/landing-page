FINAL QMD PROJECT

Tools: R, ggplot2, Plotly, RStudio

In this project, I aimed to analyze and visualize data from three different datasets—storms, electric vehicle population, and election data—to identify patterns and insights. The processes followed are as follows:

  1. Data Preparation and Cleaning:
    • Loaded the datasets: storms, countypres_2000-2020.csv (election data), and Electric_Vehicle_Population_Data.csv (electric vehicle data).
    • Cleaned the datasets by removing unnecessary columns and handling missing values. Converted state and county names to lowercase for consistency.
  2. Grouping and Summarizing Data:
    • Grouped the electric vehicle dataset by county to analyze vehicle distributions.
    • Summarized election data by state to calculate total votes.
  3. Data Visualization:
    • Created bar plots to visualize total votes by state and by candidates in the election dataset using ggplot2.
    • Generated a choropleth map to display total votes in selected states using spatial data from rnaturalearth and election data.
    • Produced violin plots to show the distribution of vehicle model years and different makes in the electric vehicle dataset.
    • Developed scatter plots to explore the relationship between vehicle model year and postal code.
  4. Geographical Mapping:
    • Focused on Canadian provinces by generating spatial data for Canada and visualized it using ggplot2.
    • Mapped storm data from the year 2010, highlighting storm tracks and locations over a map of Canada.
  5. Animation & Feature Engineering:
    • Animated storm data from 2010 using gganimate to visualize the movement and development of storms over time.
    • Created new variables to aid in data analysis and visualization, such as combining date and time information for storm data.
    • Implemented mutating functions to transform and prepare data for visualization and analysis.
  6. Final Visualization:
    • Cleaned up previous plots and integrated animations to create a comprehensive visual representation of storm data in 2010.
    • Used ggplot2 and animate to produce dynamic visualizations.
